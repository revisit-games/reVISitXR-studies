## Title of Research Study: 
Juicy Interactive Visualization: Understanding the Role of Immediate Feedback Design of Visualization Interaction

## Introduction
You are being asked to participate in a research study. Before you agree, however, you must be fully informed about the purpose of the study, the procedures to be followed, and any benefits, risks, or discomfort that you may experience as a result of your participation. This form presents information about the study so that you may make a fully informed decision regarding your participation. 

## Purpose of the Study
"Juicy" or "Juiciness" is a concept from video game development, defined as the design of immediate feedback (such as animation, visual arts, and sound effects) in relation to the interaction, to increase user engagement and the game's aesthetic appeal. Visualization (of data and/or information) is the practice of creating graphic or visual representations of data and information, to help audiences quickly understand and gain insights from complex data. The purpose of this study is to integrate the juicy design from video games into interactive data visualization graphics, and to investigate its effects on users.

## Tasks & Procedures
We are using reVISit (https://revisit.dev/) as the study and survey platform that functions similarly to Google Forms, which contains an introduction to the study, a digital consent form, the interactive graphics embedded in the website, and follow-up questionnaires to complete. When you start our study on reVISit, the first page of the reVISit study (this page) shows you the digital instructions that include explanations of the study objectives, procedures, and potential risks, a digital consent form to obtain your signature, an audio speaker test (because this study will need you to have an audio speaker to play and listen to sound clips/effects), and a color blindness test (because this study involves recognizing colored visual elements).

After obtaining the signature from you, the next section of web pages will include a series of interactive infographics/diagrams/charts developed by the investigator team. These infographics may or may not have embellishment and juicy feedback. Each interactive graphic has an instruction and tasks (for example, questions about what you may learned from the graphics). The tutorials are available via the Help button in the top-right corner of the website, accessible at any time during this study. After using each of the graphics and finishing the tasks, you will be asked about your experience of using or playing them.

Upon completing the infographics and questionnaires, you will receive a Prolific completion code on the final page.

We will review your consent form signature, graphics interaction logs/records, and questionnaire answers, and make the approval and payment in 1-2 days. At any point during the study, whether or not you have signed the consent form, you retain the right to withdraw from the study, and all of your data will be removed from any further investigation.
